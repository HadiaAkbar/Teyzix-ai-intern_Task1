Artificial intelligence is transforming the way organizations handle information. Every day, companies generate large volumes of reports, emails, articles, and internal documents that need to be reviewed by employees. Reading through all of this material manually is time-consuming and often leads to important details being overlooked. As a result, many businesses are turning to automated summarization tools to save time and improve productivity.

Document summarization is a branch of natural language processing that focuses on producing a shorter version of a text while preserving its most important information. There are two main approaches to summarization: extractive and abstractive. Extractive summarization selects the most important sentences directly from the original text and combines them to form a summary. Abstractive summarization, on the other hand, generates new sentences that capture the meaning of the original text, similar to how a human might paraphrase an article.

Extractive methods are popular because they are simple, fast, and do not require large pretrained language models. One common technique is frequency-based scoring, where the importance of a sentence is determined by how often its words appear in the document. Words that occur frequently, excluding common stopwords such as "the" and "and", are considered more important, and sentences containing many of these words receive higher scores.

Another widely used technique is TF-IDF, which stands for Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency. This method measures how important a word is to a specific sentence relative to the entire document. Words that appear often in one sentence but rarely elsewhere in the document are given higher weight. By scoring sentences based on their TF-IDF values, the summarizer can identify sentences that contain unique and meaningful information.

Beyond summarization, analytics can provide additional insight into a document. By analyzing word frequencies, it is possible to identify the most common keywords and themes in a text. Sentence importance scores can also be calculated and displayed, helping users understand which parts of the document the algorithm considered most significant.
